About Christmas

Christmas is an annual festival commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ , observed primarily on December 25th. It is a central feast day in the Christian liturgical year, followed by a season of celebration. It is a time marked by joy, goodwill, and reflection on the Christian message of hope.

Origins and Worldwide Observance

While the exact date of Christ's birth is unknown, December 25th was established in the early church. The holiday is celebrated worldwide, particularly across North America, Europe, Latin America, and Oceania. Traditions often blend religious rites with cultural practices inherited from pre-Christian winter festivals.

Traditions and Symbols

Key traditions include attending Midnight Mass or special church services, singing Christmas carols, preparing large feasts, and exchanging gifts. Popular symbols are the decorated Christmas tree, Santa Claus (Saint Nicholas), the nativity scene, and lighted candles representing the star of Bethlehem.
