About Easter

Easter is the most important and oldest festival of the Christian Church. It commemorates the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, three days after his crucifixion. It marks the culmination of the Passion of Christ, followed by a season of celebration and joy.

Date and Liturgical Significance

Easter is a movable feast, falling on the first Sunday after the full moon following the vernal equinox. It represents the foundation of Christian faith: the triumph over sin and death, and the promise of new life. It follows the solemn period of Lent.

Traditions and Symbols

Key traditions include special church services, large family meals, and the exchange of gifts. Popular symbols are the cross (representing sacrifice), Easter eggs (symbolizing new life/resurrection), the Easter lily, and the Easter bunny, which reflects spring celebrations.
