Eid al-Fitr

End of Ramadan (Islamic calendar)

About Eid al-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr, the "Festival of Breaking the Fast," is a major Muslim holiday marking the conclusion of the holy month of Ramadan . It is a day of joy, celebration, prayer, and gratitude to Allah for the strength to complete the month-long fast. It lasts for one to three days, depending on the country.

Significance and History

The festival was instituted by the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) upon his migration to Medina. It is fundamentally a day of religious achievement, acknowledging the spiritual discipline gained through fasting, reflection, and good deeds during Ramadan.

Traditions and Practices

The day begins with a special Eid prayer in congregation. Key traditions include hosting large family feasts , exchanging gifts and greetings ( Eid Mubarak ), wearing new clothes , and giving mandatory charity ( Zakat al-Fitr ) before the prayers to ensure the poor can also celebrate.
