About Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most significant festivals in Hinduism. It symbolizes the spiritual victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. The festival lasts for five days, with the main celebration occurring on the third day.

Historical Background

The origins of Diwali are rooted in ancient Indian history and mythology. According to Hindu tradition, Diwali marks the return of Lord Rama to his kingdom Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana. The people of Ayodhya lit oil lamps to welcome him back, starting the tradition of lighting diyas during Diwali.

Cultural Significance

Diwali holds immense cultural importance across India and other countries. It brings families together, promotes the exchange of gifts and sweets, and encourages charitable giving. The festival also marks the beginning of the financial year for many businesses.
