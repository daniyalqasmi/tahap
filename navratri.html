About Navratri / Dussehra

Navratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to the worship of the Hindu Goddess Durga. It symbolizes the victory of good over evil. The celebration culminates on the tenth day, known as Dussehra (or Vijayadashami), which marks Lord Rama‚Äôs victory over the demon king Ravana and Goddess Durga‚Äôs triumph over the demon Mahishasura.

Historical Background

The festival is rooted in two major legends. Navratri celebrates the nine forms of Goddess Durga, who fought and ultimately defeated the powerful buffalo demon, Mahishasura, after a nine-day battle. Dussehra commemorates the day Lord Rama killed Ravana, signifying the triumph of Dharma (righteousness) over Adharma (unrighteousness).

Cultural Significance and Traditions

Navratri is characterized by fasting and community events like Garba and Dandiya Raas, folk dances performed in honor of the Goddess. Dussehra features large processions and the burning of effigies of Ravana, Meghnada, and Kumbhakarna. It is considered an auspicious time for new beginnings, celebrating cultural unity and spiritual devotion.
