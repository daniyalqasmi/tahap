Ramadan

Ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar

About Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth and holiest month in the Islamic calendar. It is a period of intense religious observance, characterized by Sawm (fasting) from dawn until sunset. It is considered a time for deep spiritual growth, repentance, and dedication to worship and community service.

Significance and History

Ramadan is the month in which Muslims believe the first verses of the Qur'an were revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) . It aims to teach self-discipline, sacrifice, and empathy for the less fortunate. The practice of fasting is one of the Five Pillars of Islam.

Traditions and Practices

During Ramadan, Muslims wake up before dawn for a pre-fast meal ( Suhoor ) and break the fast after sunset with the evening meal ( Iftar ). Taraweeh (special nightly prayers) are performed after the evening prayer. It is also a time for increased charity and reciting the entire Qur'an.
