Dhul Hijjah 10th (Islamic calendar)

About Eid al-Adha

Eid al-Adha, the "Festival of the Sacrifice," is the second major Muslim holiday. It is celebrated annually on the 10th day of Dhul Hijjah, the last month of the Islamic lunar calendar, and coincides with the conclusion of the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca.

Significance and History

The festival commemorates Prophet Ibrahim‚Äôs (Abraham's) profound devotion and obedience when he was willing to sacrifice his son, Ismail, as commanded by Allah. This act is the basis for the sacrifice ritual, symbolizing complete submission to God's will.

Traditions and Practices

The central tradition is the Qurbani (animal sacrifice) . The meat is strictly divided into three parts: one for the family, one for friends/relatives, and one for the poor. Other traditions include Eid prayers, giving charity, and major family gatherings where food is shared.
